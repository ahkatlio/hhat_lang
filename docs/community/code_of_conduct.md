# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a friendly, safe, and welcoming environment for all, regardless of level of experience, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, personal appearance, body size, race, ethnicity, age, religion, nationality, or other similar characteristics.

## Core Principle

**We coexist in the same world. So be nice to others as you expect others to be nice to you :)**

This simple principle guides all interactions in the H-hat community.

## Expected Behavior

We expect all community members to:

### Be Respectful

- Treat everyone with respect and kindness
- Value diverse perspectives and experiences
- Accept constructive criticism gracefully
- Focus on what is best for the community
- Show empathy toward other community members

### Be Inclusive

- Welcome newcomers warmly
- Help others learn and grow
- Use inclusive language
- Make space for different viewpoints
- Recognize that everyone starts somewhere

### Be Collaborative

- Work together toward common goals
- Share knowledge generously
- Give credit where credit is due
- Assume good intentions
- Resolve disagreements constructively

### Be Professional

- Stay on topic in technical discussions
- Provide helpful, accurate information
- Admit when you don't know something
- Learn from mistakes
- Maintain professional boundaries

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not acceptable in our community:

- **Harassment**: Including offensive comments, unwelcome attention, stalking, or intimidation
- **Discrimination**: Based on any personal characteristic
- **Trolling**: Deliberately inflammatory or disruptive behavior
- **Spam**: Unsolicited advertising or off-topic content
- **Doxxing**: Publishing private information without consent
- **Threats**: Any form of threatening behavior
- **Inappropriate Content**: Including but not limited to sexual, violent, or hateful content

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- GitHub repositories and issues
- Discord channels
- Social media interactions related to H-hat
- Any other H-hat community spaces
- Representing H-hat in public spaces

## Technical Discussions

### Constructive Criticism

When reviewing code or discussing technical decisions:

- Focus on the work, not the person
- Explain the reasoning behind your suggestions
- Be specific and actionable in feedback
- Acknowledge good work and improvements
- Assume competence and good intentions

### Disagreements

Technical disagreements are normal and healthy:

- Disagree respectfully
- Focus on facts and evidence
- Stay open to changing your mind
- Escalate constructively when needed
- Accept decisions once made

## AI Contributions

As stated in our contributing guidelines:

- AI-assisted contributions are allowed
- You are responsible for all code you submit
- Simply prompting AI without critical review is not acceptable
- Understand and verify any AI-generated code
- Do not submit AI-generated content without evaluation

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

1. **Minor Issues**: Consider addressing directly if you feel comfortable
2. **Serious Issues**: Contact project maintainers privately
3. **Urgent Issues**: Report immediately to Discord moderators or GitHub

All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality.

### Consequences

Project maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate, including:

- Warning the offender
- Temporary ban from community spaces
- Permanent ban from community spaces
- Reporting to relevant platforms (Discord, GitHub)

### Appeals

If you believe you were unfairly sanctioned, you may appeal by:

- Contacting a different maintainer privately
- Explaining your perspective calmly
- Providing context we may have missed

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:

- Clarifying standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate corrective action
- Enforcing the Code of Conduct fairly and consistently
- Leading by example

Maintainers who do not follow the Code of Conduct may face temporary or permanent repercussions.

## Questions and Concerns

### About This Code of Conduct

If you have questions about this Code of Conduct:

- Ask on Discord in `#h-hat`
- Open a GitHub Discussion
- Contact a maintainer privately

### About Behavior

If you're unsure whether something is appropriate:

- **When in doubt, ask first**
- Err on the side of respect and inclusion
- Consider how you would feel receiving the comment
